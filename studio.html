Song|Flow — Lyric Studio · Guide

The idea

Every bar is built from two things: rhyming vowels (the anchors) and the consonants between them (the rhythm). Rhyme = the same vowel sound landing on the same spot across bars. The whole tool is here to help you place those anchors and fill the rhythm between them.

Writing flow

  • Gutter (left numbers): each line shows bar № │ syllables VOWEL — the vowel is that line's ending rhyme sound.
  • Bar guidance (right panel): on even bars it tells you to complete the couplet — match the anchor vowel and syllable count of the bar above. On odd bars it offers fresh, contrasting vowels to open a new scheme. It warns once a scheme runs past 8 bars.
  • Cross-rhyme (ABAB): a rhyme doesn't have to answer the very next bar. When you set vowel A on bar 1, a different vowel B on bar 2, then answer A on bar 3 and B on bar 4, you get an alternating (cross) rhyme — the pair lands every other bar. Because the pattern repeats, even a loose / family rhyme (e.g. shoes→clothes→…→phone) reads as deliberate and satisfying. Once the tool sees an A-B-A run it prompts the B answer, and the scheme map rings the every-other-bar pair in white.
  • Rhyme countdown: as you type, a small chip follows your cursor showing how many syllables until your next rhyme should land, and which vowel to hit. It counts down and disappears when you reach it.
  • 🌈 Rhyme colors: end-words that rhyme share a color; dotted underlines mark internal echoes.
  • Word chips: Near/Slant rhymes and the guidance chips — click to copy a word.

Shaping & generating

  • Force rhyming vowel(s) (in Generate): an optional override, off by default. Flip a switch to make the generator land every new bar on a specific vowel — even when it breaks the natural scheme. For deliberate effects/special cases; leave off to let the scheme lead.
  • Generation feel (the pad): drag the dot. Left↔right sets phrasing density (packed ↔ sustained); up↔down sets how strictly it stays on-theme vs. lets sound lead the meter. Lower = looser meaning buys a tighter rhythm; the app then enforces matching syllables on paired bars and re-asks the AI if a pair drifts.
  • Generate matching lyrics: writes new bars that extend your scheme. Pick an Engine: a free hosted open model, or bring your own Anthropic / OpenAI / Google key (stored only in this browser — you pay that provider). Set the key with the 🔑 Key button and choose a model.
  • Export .txt: save the document with its title, BPM and key.

Arrangement timeline

  • One tag, two names. A [tag] with lyrics under it is a Lyrics Region tag in the editor; the same tag shows on the timeline as an arrangement region tag. Two views of one thing. A tag can also carry no lyrics (e.g. [Instrumental break]): it still appears in the editor, you just give it a bar length.
  • Bar length. Tags with lyrics size themselves automatically (1 line = 1 bar). For tags without lyrics, set the length from the tag menu (4 / 8 / 16 / 32 / custom). Open the menu by clicking the tag in the lyric editor, or Alt+Click the region on the timeline. The menu also has Rename and Delete.
  • Beat grid & Anchor. The grid is synced to the BPM. To lock it to your song: move the play marker to the first downbeat (turn Snap off for precision), then press ⚓ Anchor — bar 1 (and the metronome) snap to the marker and the grid pivots from there. Bars before the anchor are numbered 0, −1, −2… so you can see the lead-in.
  • Slip is an offset relative to the anchor (neutral 0 = right on it). Nudge with ◀ ▶ (±10ms, Shift = ±1ms); for a big shift, toggle the ⇄ slip-drag button and drag the numbered ruler. Click the readout to reset to neutral.
  • Snap. The playhead and selections snap to the grid. Pick the resolution next to the Snap toggle: whole note (default) / ½ / ¼ (beat) / ⅛ / 1⁄16.
  • Region colors. A region that contains lyrics shows teal; an arrangement-only region with no lyrics (e.g. [Instrumental break]) shows amber.
  • Drag a region to reorder it — an insert line shows where it lands, and the rest ripple aside. Ctrl+Click several, then drag to move them as a block.
  • Selecting with gaps: Ctrl+Click two non-adjacent regions and only those highlight on the waveform — the regions between them stay un-highlighted. They get auto-added only when you run an action that needs a continuous run: Loop selected, or Copy / Cut / Duplicate.
  • 🔁 Loop selected loops the highlighted regions (they turn red, gaps filled). 🗑 Delete region (or the Delete key) removes a region; lyric-bearing ones warn first, and Ctrl+Z undoes it.
  • Transport keys: Space = play / stop (the marker returns to where playback started). Enter = play / pause in place (the marker stays put). The ▶ button pauses in place too.
  • ▣ Scratch pad: a composite lane for chopping audio — closed by default. Hit ▣ Scratch to open it, copy a waveform span, and paste it in to audition or export. Close it anytime.
  • Resize: drag the handle just under the timeline to make the waveform taller. 🔔 Click = metronome. ☰ Auto sections detects sections from a loaded track.

Audio editing & inpaint

  • Right-click the timeline → Add region → 4 / 8 / 16 bars inserts that many blank bars and ripple-pushes the rest of the song to the right (nothing is deleted). Re-fit your [tags] afterward — audio edits don't move them.
  • The 🎨 Inpaint panel opens on the new region: type a genre/vibe and pick a model (ACE-Step by default). The fill renders on the Songflow audio engine (a cloud worker) — until that's connected the region stays silent; everything else is fully offline.

Everything runs in your browser — nothing is uploaded. Documents autosave here.
